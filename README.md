# 🏨 Hôtel "De l'étoile" - Revenue Management Crisis Intervention

## 📌 Executive Summary
**Hôtel De l'étoile** is experiencing a significant decline in profitability, marked by dropping RevPAR and shifting market segments. This project, originally a capstone for the ESSEC Business School Revenue Management specialization, analyzes historical booking data to diagnose the root causes of the crisis and proposes a data-driven turnaround strategy focusing on dynamic pricing, channel optimization, and inventory control.

## 🧰 Tools & Techniques Used
*   **Data Visualization:** Microsoft Power BI (DAX, Power Query)
*   **Data Manipulation:** Advanced Excel (Pivot Tables, VLOOKUP, Statistical functions)
*   **Revenue Metrics Analyzed:** ADR, RevPAR, TRevPAR, GOPPAR, Distribution Costs, Lead Time.

## 📊 The Business Problem
Despite maintaining high occupancy rates during certain periods, the hotel's net revenue is shrinking. The objective of this analysis is to answer three critical questions:
1.  Which market segments are cannibalizing our high-yield bookings?
2.  How are third-party OTA commissions impacting our Net RevPAR?
3.  What pricing barriers (rate fences) need to be implemented immediately?

*   **Insight 1 (Segmentation):** Corporate segment bookings have dropped by X%, while highly discounted Leisure bookings have surged, lowering the overall ADR.
*   **Insight 2 (Distribution):** Reliance on OTA channels has increased commission costs by X%, severely impacting GOPPAR despite stable gross revenue.
*   **Insight 3 (Lead Time):** The hotel is filling up too early with low-rate transient guests, leaving no availability for high-yield, short-lead-time corporate travelers.

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations
Based on the data, I developed the following Revenue Management interventions:
1.  **Implement Strict Rate Fences:** Introduce minimum length of stay (MinLOS) restrictions during peak compression dates to prioritize high-yield bookings.
2.  **Channel Shift Strategy:** Launch a direct-booking incentive program to reduce OTA dependency and recover X% in commission fees.
3.  **Dynamic Pricing Matrix:** Restructure the pricing tiers based on a newly forecasted unconstrained demand curve.
